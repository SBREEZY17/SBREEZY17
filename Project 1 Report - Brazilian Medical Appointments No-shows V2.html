Introduction¶

This dataset contains information about patients in Brazil and whether or not they showed up for their scheduled doctors appointments.
The aim of the study is to find possible links between patient data and whether it increases the liklihood of a no-show.

To do that, I will look to answer the following questions:

  • Most common amount of days between appt and schd for no shows?
  • Most common age for no shows?
  • Does disability/handicap create more no shows?
  • Which gender no shows more often?
  • Does having a scholarship make you no show less?
  • Most common neighbourhood for no shows?
  • Does receiving the SMS help?

The graph shows that of those people who received an SMS, how many of them were no show/show and how many days did they have between making the appt and day of the apppointment.

No SMS were sent out for appointments made 2 days or less before.

If apppointments were made 3-4 or 5-10 days before and the person received an SMS, they were more likely to show up to their appointment.

If appointments were made 11-30 or more than 30 days before, even though they received an SMS, they were more likely to no show.

This suggests that sending an SMS is most effective for patients booking between 3-10 days before the scheduled date. Although, as we don't know when the SMS is sent, we can't make a comment on how many days before the appointment the SMS is most effective; this would require more data.

Conclusion¶

After running the above analysis, we can deduce that the most interesting areas for further research would be time between making the appointment and the scheduled date and items such as age group and neighbourhood, which I did not look into in enough detail.

Since most appointments are made on the same day and this category has the least amount of no shows, we can guess that this type of appointment is urgent or highly needed.

Appointments made more than 5 days in advance is the key area for further research.

There are some limitations of this data, which could be improved for better analysis:

  • We do not know when the SMS was sent, is that we cannot measure it's effectiveness in terms of reminding the patient to attend the appointment in terms of 'when is the best time to remind a patient?'.

  • The data is also only collected over a few months in one year. If we had more months or years, we could observe patterns
